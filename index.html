This month is all about waking up your body. Every exercise is gentle and introductory. You'll build the habits and posture that skating demands ‚Äî no rushing, no pain. 3 sets of each exercise unless noted.

Equipment This Month

Just your 5 lb dumbbell and a yoga mat or soft floor. No other equipment needed ‚Äî this month is all about learning movement patterns with your bodyweight.

Weekly Schedule ‚Äî Repeat All 4 Weeks (increasing reps by 2 each week)
Monday ‚Äî Lower Body & Balance Intro
Warm-Up
5 min easy march in place, ankle circles, gentle leg swings holding a wall
Exercise 1
Wall squats ‚Äî back flat on wall, slide down until thighs are parallel. Hold 10 sec, stand. 8 reps. This teaches the bent-knee position used constantly in skating.
Exercise 2
Standing calf raises ‚Äî hold wall for balance, rise onto toes slowly, lower slowly. 15 reps. Essential for push-off power.
Exercise 3
Single-leg stand ‚Äî hold wall lightly, lift one foot 2 inches off floor, hold 10 seconds each side. 3 rounds. The foundational skating balance skill.
Exercise 4
Bodyweight squats ‚Äî feet hip-width, sit back like sitting into a chair, stand up. 10 reps. Focus on keeping chest up and knees over toes.
Cool-Down
5 min: seated hamstring stretch, quad stretch holding wall, calf stretch on step
Tuesday ‚Äî Core & Posture Foundations
Warm-Up
5 min: arm circles, torso rotations, cat-cow stretches on hands and knees
Exercise 1
Dead bug ‚Äî lie on back, arms up, knees bent at 90¬∞. Slowly lower one arm and the opposite leg toward floor without touching, return. 8 each side. Trains the deep core stabilizers skaters rely on.
Exercise 2
Wall angels ‚Äî stand with back, head, and arms flat on wall. Slowly slide arms up and down like a snow angel. 10 reps. Fixes rounded shoulders and builds skating posture.
Exercise 3
Glute bridges ‚Äî lie on back, feet flat, lift hips until body is a straight line. Hold 3 sec, lower. 12 reps. Builds the glutes that drive every skating push.
Exercise 4
Knee plank hold ‚Äî on hands and knees, lower to forearms, hold a straight line from knees to head. 15‚Äì20 sec holds, 3 times. Your first plank ‚Äî build the habit.
Cool-Down
Child's pose, chest opener on floor, gentle neck stretches
Wednesday ‚Äî Flexibility & Body Awareness
Warm-Up
5 min light movement, gentle joint circles from ankles to shoulders
Stretch 1
Hip flexor lunge stretch ‚Äî one knee on floor, other foot forward. Gently push hips forward. Hold 30 sec each side. Tight hip flexors are one of the biggest limiters for new skaters.
Stretch 2
Seated straddle ‚Äî sit with legs wide apart, gently lean forward with flat back. Hold 45 sec. Builds inner thigh and hip flexibility for wider skating stance.
Stretch 3
Figure-4 stretch ‚Äî lie on back, cross one ankle over opposite knee, pull both toward chest. Hold 30 sec each side. Opens the hips for rotation in jumps and spins.
Stretch 4
Doorway chest stretch ‚Äî place forearms on doorframe, lean gently through. Hold 30 sec. Opens the chest for the upright posture skaters need.
Stretch 5
Ankle alphabet ‚Äî seated, lift one foot, draw the alphabet with your toes. Both feet. Builds the ankle mobility crucial for edges and footwork.
Thursday ‚Äî Arms, Posture & Position Practice
Warm-Up
5 min: shoulder rolls, arm swings, gentle upper back stretches
Exercise 1
Dumbbell lateral raises (5 lb) ‚Äî stand tall, raise arms out to sides to shoulder height, lower slowly. 10 reps. Builds the shoulder strength to hold beautiful arm positions.
Exercise 2
Skating arm position holds ‚Äî stand with feet together, hold arms in an oval skating position (like hugging a beach ball). Hold 30 sec without dropping. Trains arm muscle memory.
Exercise 3
Dumbbell bent-over row ‚Äî hinge at hips, pull 5lb weight to hip with elbow back. 10 each arm. Builds back strength for upright skating posture.
Exercise 4
Modified push-ups ‚Äî from knees, lower chest to floor and press up. 8 reps. Beginner-friendly chest and arm strength.
Cool-Down
Shoulder cross-body stretch, tricep stretch, wrist circles
Friday ‚Äî Full Body & Skating Simulation
Warm-Up
5 min: march in place, hip circles, arm swings
Exercise 1
Skater stance hold ‚Äî bend knees softly, lean slightly forward from hips (not waist), arms in oval. Hold 20 sec. This is the actual skating ready position ‚Äî get comfortable here.
Exercise 2
Side step squats ‚Äî in squat position, step sideways 4 steps left, 4 steps right. 3 rounds. Mimics the lateral movement of skating strides.
Exercise 3
Balance reach ‚Äî stand on one leg, slowly reach other leg forward, to side, and behind. 5 reaches each direction per leg. Builds the controlled balance used on every edge.
Exercise 4
Spiral introduction ‚Äî hold wall, slowly lift one leg behind you as high as comfortable, hold 5 sec. 5 each leg. Your first practice of the iconic skating spiral position.
Cool-Down
10 min full body stretch ‚Äî spend extra time on anything that feels tight
Saturday & Sunday ‚Äî Rest & Recovery
Saturday
Optional: 20 min gentle walk or easy yoga. Watch figure skating videos and notice skaters' posture, arm positions, and body lines.
Sunday
Full rest. Muscles grow during rest, not during training ‚Äî this is when your work pays off. Gentle stretching while watching TV is fine.
Month 1 ‚Äî Key Exercises Explained
Wall Squat
3 √ó 8 reps, 10 sec hold
Teaches the bent-knee "ready position" that is the foundation of all skating. Back flat on wall keeps your form honest.
Dead Bug
3 √ó 8 each side
Activates deep core stabilizers without straining the back. The anti-rotation demand mirrors what your core does on every skating edge.
Single-Leg Stand
3 √ó 10 sec each leg
Nearly all skating happens on one foot at a time. This humble exercise is foundational ‚Äî master it before anything else.
Glute Bridge
3 √ó 12 reps
The glutes are the engine of skating. Every push, every landing, every edge relies on strong glutes. This gentle exercise wakes them up.
Hip Flexor Stretch
Hold 30 sec √ó 3
Tight hip flexors tilt the pelvis forward and ruin skating posture. Releasing them is one of the most impactful things a beginner can do.
Skater Stance Hold
3 √ó 20 sec holds
Practice the actual skating posture ‚Äî knees bent, slight forward lean from hips, arms out. Your body needs to memorize this feeling.
Month 1 ‚Äî Coaching Notes
  • If any exercise feels painful (not just hard), stop and rest. Muscle fatigue is expected; joint pain is not.
  • In Week 1, add 2 reps to everything. Week 2, add 2 more. Week 3, add 2 more. Week 4, aim for the Week 3 numbers with better form.
  • Film yourself doing the skater stance hold and compare your posture to a skater on video ‚Äî you'll learn a lot.
  • Go slow on every single-leg exercise. Speed cheats balance. Slow forces your stabilizers to actually engage.
  • The ankle alphabet seems silly but is genuinely one of the best things you can do for skating. Do it every day, even while watching TV.
02
Build
"Develop strength, range of motion, and single-leg control"
Single-leg strength Deeper flexibility Core stability Jump landing prep

You've built your base. Now we add challenge ‚Äî harder variations, longer holds, and more skating-specific movements. Exercises progress from "learning the pattern" to "building strength in the pattern." Add resistance bands this month if possible.

Recommended New Equipment

A set of light resistance bands (~$12) is strongly recommended this month. They're the single most useful skating training tool ‚Äî used for hip strengthening, ankle work, and position resistance. A yoga mat if you don't have one.

Weekly Schedule ‚Äî Increase reps/duration by ~10% each week
Monday ‚Äî Single-Leg Strength
Warm-Up
8 min: dynamic leg swings, hip circles, 2 min light jog in place
Exercise 1
Assisted single-leg squat ‚Äî hold chair, lower on one leg until other foot touches floor lightly, stand back up. 8 each leg. A huge step up from two-legged squats ‚Äî the knee tracking matters!
Exercise 2
Lateral band walks (if you have bands) or wide sumo squat walks ‚Äî step sideways in a squat position, 10 steps each direction. 3 rounds. Directly targets hip abductors that control every skating edge.
Exercise 3
Step-up ‚Äî use a sturdy step or stair, step up with right foot, bring left foot up, step back down. 12 each leg. Adds functional single-leg power without jumping yet.
Exercise 4
Single-leg calf raises ‚Äî hold wall, rise up on one foot only. 12 each leg. Much harder than two-legged, mimics the toe push in skating.
Cool-Down
10 min lower body stretching, focusing on inner thighs and glutes
Tuesday ‚Äî Core Stability & Rotation
Warm-Up
5 min: cat-cow, bird dog warmup, torso rotations standing
Exercise 1
Full plank hold ‚Äî now on toes instead of knees. Hold 20‚Äì30 sec. If this is too hard, do 15 sec full, then drop to knees. Build the real plank this month.
Exercise 2
Standing wood chops (5 lb dumbbell) ‚Äî hold weight with both hands, rotate from shoulder height diagonally down to opposite hip. 12 each side. Builds rotational core power used in all jumps.
Exercise 3
Bird dog with hold ‚Äî from hands and knees, extend opposite arm and leg, hold 5 sec, return. 10 each side. Same as Month 1 but now hold it still ‚Äî challenge the balance harder.
Exercise 4
Glute bridge march ‚Äî in bridge position, slowly lift one foot 3 inches off floor, lower, switch sides while keeping hips level. 10 each leg. Much harder than the static bridge!
Cool-Down
Spine twist stretch, cobra pose, child's pose
Wednesday ‚Äî Deep Flexibility
Warm-Up
5 min: full body gentle movement, get warm before stretching deeply
Stretch 1
Low lunge with twist ‚Äî in lunge position, reach arm toward ceiling and rotate. Hold 30 sec each side. Opens hip flexors and thoracic spine together ‚Äî both critical for skating.
Stretch 2
Pigeon pose ‚Äî from plank, bring one knee forward behind wrist, lower onto forearms if possible. Hold 45 sec‚Äì1 min each side. The gold standard hip opener.
Stretch 3
Frog stretch ‚Äî on hands and knees, slowly slide knees apart as wide as comfortable, lower to forearms. Hold 60 sec. Opens inner thighs for wider stance and crossovers.
Stretch 4
Standing quad stretch with balance ‚Äî without holding wall, bend one knee and hold ankle behind you. Balance 20 sec each side. Combines flexibility and balance practice.
Stretch 5
Shoulder + back opening ‚Äî seated, cross one arm, thread needle under body. Works the thoracic rotation needed for upper body presentation.
Thursday ‚Äî Upper Body & Arm Training
Warm-Up
5 min: arm circles, shoulder rolls, light dumbbell warmup movements
Exercise 1
Full push-up ‚Äî progress from knees to toes this month, even if just 3‚Äì4 reps before dropping to knees. 3 sets working toward 8 full reps. Arm strength matters more than you'd think for spin entry and jump takeoff.
Exercise 2
Arm position endurance ‚Äî hold arms in skating oval for 45 sec, transition to arabesque arm position (one arm forward, one back), hold 30 sec. 3 rounds. Your arms must hold positions throughout a program without drooping.
Exercise 3
Dumbbell shoulder press (5 lb) ‚Äî seated or standing, press both arms overhead from shoulder height. 12 reps. Builds overhead strength for future overhead lifts and lift positions.
Exercise 4
Superman hold ‚Äî lie face down, lift arms and legs off floor simultaneously, hold 5 sec. 10 reps. Builds back extensors needed for the layback and camel positions.
Cool-Down
Chest stretch, shoulder stretch, wrist and forearm release
Friday ‚Äî Jump Preparation & Power
Warm-Up
8 min: jog in place, high knees, butt kicks, leg swings ‚Äî get fully warm before jumping!
Exercise 1
Jump squat with landing hold ‚Äî squat down, jump up, land softly and hold the landing position for 3 sec. 10 reps. The landing position (one leg bent, arms in, opposite leg back) is the most important skill you're building for jumping.
Exercise 2
Lateral bounds ‚Äî jump sideways from one foot to the other, sticking the landing for 2 sec before jumping back. 8 each side. Builds the explosive lateral power of the skating stride.
Exercise 3
Tuck position practice ‚Äî stand, then rapidly pull arms and one knee to your chest (like the midair position of a jump), hold balance. 10 each leg. Training air position muscle memory before you're actually airborne on skates.
Exercise 4
Spiral hold without wall ‚Äî stand freely, slowly lift one leg behind as high as comfortable, hold for 10 sec. 4 each leg. Progress from Month 1's wall-supported version.
Cool-Down
10 min: full body stretch, pay attention to anything that's sore from jumping
Saturday & Sunday ‚Äî Rest & Active Recovery
Saturday
Optional: 20‚Äì30 min yoga for athletes, or a brisk walk. Review your Month 1 progress ‚Äî can you feel improvement in your balance? Your squat depth?
Sunday
Rest. Foam roll if you have one. Practice arm positions while sitting on the couch ‚Äî it's more helpful than it seems!
Month 2 ‚Äî Key Progressions
Assisted Single-Leg Squat
3 √ó 8 each leg
Progresses the wall squat to real single-leg loading. Almost all skating movement is single-leg. This is the most skating-specific strength exercise you can do.
Jump Landing Hold
3 √ó 10 reps
You won't be jumping on the ice for a while, but building the landing position now means your body will know it when you arrive. Soft knees, controlled, quiet landing.
Lateral Band Walks
3 √ó 10 steps each way
The hip abductors drive every skating push. This is one of the most direct off-ice exercises for that specific muscle group, used constantly in edge work.
Wood Chops
3 √ó 12 each side
The rotational power of figure skating jumps comes from the obliques and core rotation. This exercise directly trains that diagonal force pattern.
Pigeon Pose
Hold 45‚Äì60 sec each
One of the most effective hip openers in existence. Deep hip flexibility dramatically improves the quality of spirals, spins, and the overall look of skating.
Free Spiral Hold
4 √ó 10 sec each leg
Progression from the wall-supported Month 1 version. The spiral is an iconic skating element ‚Äî building it off-ice means you'll have head start on the ice.
Month 2 ‚Äî Coaching Notes
  • On the single-leg squat, watch your knee ‚Äî it should track over your second toe, not cave inward. Use a mirror.
  • When landing jump squats, the sound matters ‚Äî aim for silent, soft landings. Loud thumping landings are exactly what judges see on the ice.
  • If you can't do a full push-up yet, that's completely fine ‚Äî partial reps (going halfway down) still build strength.
  • The frog stretch and pigeon pose may be uncomfortable ‚Äî that's normal for tight hips. Breathe deeply into the stretch; never force it.
  • Try to get resistance bands this month. The lateral walk alone is worth the investment for skating hip development.

Two months of foundations pay off here. Training intensity rises noticeably. Sessions get longer, exercises get harder, and your body is now ready to handle real athletic training. You'll also add stamina work ‚Äî skating is cardiovascularly demanding.

If you can, a heavier dumbbell (8‚Äì10 lb) will enhance several exercises this month. A balance pad or folded yoga mat under your feet adds balance challenge. Jump rope ($8) is great for cardio that mimics skating rhythm.

Weekly Schedule ‚Äî Intensity increases each week, track your personal bests

Warm-Up
10 min: jog, dynamic stretches, bodyweight squats √ó 15, hip circles
Exercise 1
Bulgarian split squat (5‚Äì10 lb dumbbell) ‚Äî rear foot on chair, lower back knee toward floor. 10 each leg. One of the best single-leg strength builders ‚Äî your thighs will feel it!

Plank with arm reach ‚Äî in full plank, slowly extend one arm forward without rotating hips. 10 each arm. Anti-rotation core demand that mirrors the upper/lower body independence in skating.

V-sit hold ‚Äî sit with legs and torso lifted in a V shape, hold 15‚Äì20 sec. 4 rounds. Builds the compact core strength used in the sit spin position.

3 √ó 10 each leg
The king of single-leg skating strength. Rear foot elevated creates a deep range of motion that directly translates to powerful skating strides and controlled landings.

Month 3 ‚Äî Coaching Notes

  • For skating positions (scratch spin, sit spin, spiral), practice in front of a mirror or film yourself. Compare your line to skaters on video.
  • Cardio day is not optional ‚Äî cardiovascular fitness is as important as strength for skating. A jump skater who's winded can't execute clean landings.
  • If the full push-up is still difficult, keep at it ‚Äî by the end of this month most people can do 5‚Äì8. Consistency over perfection.
  • You may notice your ankle mobility has improved significantly since Month 1. Test it ‚Äî can you do a deeper plie? That's real progress.

"Train like a skater ‚Äî you're ready for the ice"

The final month. Training mirrors what competitive figure skaters do off-season. You're not a beginner anymore ‚Äî you have balance, strength, flexibility, and skating position knowledge. This month finalizes your readiness to step on the ice with real capability.

Use everything you have ‚Äî dumbbells (upgrade to 8‚Äì10 lb where possible), resistance bands, and a balance surface. This month you'll also do a full "mock program run-through" ‚Äî a sequence of movements done continuously for 2‚Äì3 minutes to simulate skating a program.
